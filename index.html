Notes for "Never Let Me Go"

Setting Characters Themes Questions Important Moments Additional Sections

Setting

Hailsham: A secluded boarding school in the English countryside. It fosters a tranquil environment with its expansive fields, gardens, and art classes. Despite its idyllic setting, there’s an ominous undercurrent; students are subtly prepared for a mysterious, predefined future.

The Cottages: Rural buildings introduced as a future destination. Former Hailsham students live semi-autonomous lives here before stepping into society. Little is revealed initially, adding to the novel's mystery.

Characters

Kathy H.: The protagonist and narrator, reflective and observant.

Tommy: A temperamental but kind boy, struggles with self-expression.

Ruth: Charismatic and controlling, yet insecure.

Miss Lucy: A teacher who occasionally reveals uncomfortable truths about Hailsham.

Madame: Mysterious figure connected to the "gallery."

Themes

Identity and Purpose: Students questioning their role in society.

Friendship and Power Dynamics: Complex relationships between Kathy, Ruth, and Tommy.

Ethics and Humanity: Subtle hints at the moral questions surrounding the students' existence.

Questions

Why are the students’ health and art so closely monitored?

What is the purpose of Hailsham’s emphasis on creativity?

Who is Madame, and what is the significance of the gallery?

Important Moments

Tommy’s tantrum and Miss Lucy’s reassurance about creativity.

Ruth giving Kathy the pencil case and its implications for their friendship.

Kathy discovering Madame’s reaction to the students.

Additional Sections

Speculations about the guardians’ cryptic behavior.

Kathy’s reflections on small moments that reveal bigger truths.
